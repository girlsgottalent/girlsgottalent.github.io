In the last picture, you can see the individual waves of sound. A little bit of backstory: sound exists as waves, shaped like ocean waves but made up of, well, sound! They vary in several different ways:

  • How tall they are means how loud they are
  • How close together they are means how high the pitch is (closer = higher pitch)
  • There are other things like sound pressure that we can't see from the graph alone.

Beyond that, different instruments look different when they're graphed, and even different pianos or different violins or certainly different voices look different!

If you want to learn even more about sound, Columbia University has a nice tutorial on the basic physics of sound and some more examples of different sounds and their graphs.
