What they used:

Cup 1:

  • water (H2O)
  • starch packet
  • citric acid (C6H8O7)
  • sodium sulfite (Na2SO3)

Cup 2:

  • water (H2O)
  • sodium iodate (NaIO3)

What's happening:

The starch packet and citric acid are in battle. At first, when the two cups are mixed, the sodium sulfite and sodium iodate work together to make iodine. The starch is trying to turn iodine blue, but citric acid keeps it clear. Eventually the citric acid runs out and the mixture turns blue!
